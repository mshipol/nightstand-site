1. What we collect

Account information

When you create an account, we collect:

  • Your email address
  • Your display name (optional)
  • Profile photo (optional, if you upload one)
  • Authentication method (email/password, Google Sign-In, or Sign in with Apple)

Reading data

The core purpose of the app — we store:

  • Books you add to your library (title, author, cover, page count, status)
  • Reading sessions (start time, end time, pages read or minutes listened)
  • Reading goals, notes, ratings, and shelf assignments
  • Badges and achievements you've earned
  • Goodreads import data (if you choose to import)

Usage data

We collect anonymized analytics to understand how the app is used and where to improve:

  • Which features are used (not the content of your data)
  • App crashes and error logs
  • Device type and OS version (no device identifiers)

What we do NOT collect

  • Your precise location
  • Contacts or address book
  • Photos or camera (except the cover images you explicitly upload)
  • Microphone or audio
  • Browsing history outside of Nightstand

2. How we use your data

We use your data to:

  • Provide, maintain, and improve the Nightstand app
  • Sync your reading data across your devices
  • Send optional streak reminders and notifications (you control this in Settings)
  • Calculate your reading stats and award badges
  • Respond to support requests
  • Detect and prevent abuse or fraud

We do not use your data to:

  • Sell to third parties — ever
  • Serve third-party advertising
  • Build ad profiles or interest graphs
  • Train AI models on your personal reading data

3. Third-party services

Nightstand uses the following third-party services to function. Each has its own privacy policy.

Firebase (Google)

We use Firebase for authentication, data storage, and crash reporting. Firebase Auth handles sign-in, Cloud Firestore stores your reading library, and Firebase Crashlytics helps us identify and fix bugs. Google's privacy policy applies: policies.google.com/privacy

RevenueCat

RevenueCat processes subscription payments and manages entitlements. RevenueCat receives a pseudonymous user ID (not your email) and your subscription state. RevenueCat's privacy policy: revenuecat.com/privacy

Google Books API & Open Library

When you search for a book, we query the Google Books API and Open Library to find metadata (title, author, cover, page count). Your search queries are sent to these services. No account information is shared.

Apple App Store / Google Play

Payment processing for Premium subscriptions is handled entirely by Apple or Google through their respective app stores. We do not see or store your payment information.

4. Data storage & security

Your data is stored in Google Cloud (Firebase) infrastructure in the United States. We use industry-standard security practices including:

  • Encrypted connections (TLS/HTTPS) for all data in transit
  • Firebase Security Rules to ensure users can only access their own data
  • Firebase Authentication for secure account management

No system is 100% secure. If you have concerns, contact us at the address below.

5. Your rights

Account deletion

You can permanently delete your Nightstand account and all associated data directly from the app:

  1. Open Nightstand → Profile tab
  2. Scroll to Settings
  3. Tap Delete Account
  4. Confirm by typing "DELETE"

Deletion is permanent and removes all your books, sessions, stats, and account data from our servers within 30 days.

Data export

You can export your reading data in the app under Profile → Settings → Export Data. This gives you a JSON file with your full reading history.

Opt out of analytics

You can opt out of anonymized analytics in Profile → Settings → Privacy.

Notification control

All notifications are optional. You can enable or disable streak reminders, badge alerts, and other notifications in Profile → Settings → Notifications.

6. Children's privacy

Nightstand is not directed at children under 13 (or under 16 in the EU/UK). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us personal information, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.
